\chapter{Introduction}
\label{chap:introduction}

\section{Background}
\label{section:background}

Road safety remains a major public health concern, especially in countries where motorcycles are the primary mode of transportation. Despite helmet laws being in place in many countries, compliance remains inconsistent, and traditional enforcement methods such as police checkpoints and manual CCTV review are limited in coverage, costly, and inefficient at scale.

Advances in artificial intelligence, particularly in computer vision and deep learning, have opened new possibilities for automating traffic law enforcement. This project, HelmTrack, proposes an AI-powered system that combines real-time helmet violation detection with automatic license plate recognition, aiming to reduce the burden on enforcement officers and provide scalable, evidence-based traffic monitoring.

\section{Problem Statement}
\label{section:problem-statement}

Motorcycle accidents are among the leading causes of traffic fatalities in many countries, and not wearing a helmet significantly increases the risk of fatal head injuries. Current enforcement relies on physical checkpoints and manual CCTV review, both of which are limited in coverage, costly in manpower, and unable to operate at a city-wide scale. As a result, violators are rarely caught outside of manned checkpoints, weakening the deterrent effect of helmet laws. There is a clear need for an automated system that can continuously monitor traffic, detect violations in real time, and generate reliable evidence without constant human oversight.

\section{Solution Overview}
\label{section:solution-overview}

HelmTrack is an AI-based real-time monitoring system that automatically detects motorcycle helmet violations and identifies offending vehicles through license plate recognition. The system processes CCTV video using a multi-stage computer vision pipeline to assess helmet compliance and extract license plate information when a violation is found. Violation records, including visual evidence, timestamp, plate number, and confidence scores, are automatically logged for authority review. The system is designed to be compatible with existing CCTV infrastructure and requires no continuous human supervision.

\subsection{Features}
\label{subsection:features}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=80pt]
    \item \textbf{Real-Time Object Detection}: Detects motorcycles, riders, helmets, and license plates from video streams using YOLOv8.
    \item \textbf{Helmet Violation Detection}: Determines whether a rider is wearing a helmet in each video frame.
    \item \textbf{Automatic License Plate Recognition}: Extracts the license plate number using OCR when a violation is detected.
    \item \textbf{Violation Evidence Logging}: Stores each violation with a frame image, plate image, plate number, timestamp, and confidence score.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Target User}
\label{section:target-user}

HelmTrack is designed for traffic law enforcement professionals and does not require any expertise in AI or computer vision to operate.

\textbf{Traffic Police Officers} are the primary users, responsible for reviewing and processing helmet violation records generated by the system. HelmTrack reduces its need for continuous manual monitoring by delivering automatically generated evidence-backed reports.

\textbf{City and Municipal Authorities} use the system to gain data-driven insights on violation rates and high-risk locations to support road safety policy decisions.

\textbf{Highway and Road Departments} benefit from the system's ability to monitor geographically dispersed roads where maintaining a permanent officer presence is impractical.
